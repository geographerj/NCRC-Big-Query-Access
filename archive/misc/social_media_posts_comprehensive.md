### Mortgage Report Part 1 - Post 2
The mortgage market has undergone dramatic structural changes since 2018. Non-bank mortgage companies now originate 53.3% of all home loans, up from 44.6% in 2018, while traditional bank market share dropped from 42.5% to just 30.1%. This shift matters because non-bank lenders are not subject to Community Reinvestment Act oversight, the key law that requires banks to serve communities historically excluded from credit.

Two-thirds of home purchase mortgages in 2024 came from non-bank lenders. Credit unions grew to 16.6% market share, also exempt from full CRA requirements. This fundamental shift in who provides mortgage credit threatens to undermine efforts to close economic opportunity gaps along race and class lines.

Total lending collapsed from 16.8 million loans in 2021 to just 4.6 million in 2023 as interest rates climbed. Refinancing fell over 90% from pandemic peaks. The 30-year mortgage rate peaked near 8% in late 2023 before moderating to 6.8% by early 2025.

States must defend residents through expanded CRA frameworks like those in Massachusetts, New York, and Illinois. We need to preserve down payment assistance programs and protect CFPB enforcement. https://ncrc.org/mortgage-market-part-1 #justeconomy #fairlending #cra #mortgages #homeownership

### Mortgage Report Part 1 - Post 4
The COVID-19 pandemic created unprecedented volatility in mortgage lending. Total loan originations more than doubled between 2019 and 2021, reaching historic peaks of 16.8 million loans driven by interest rates below 3%. As rates climbed post-pandemic, lending contracted sharply to 6.7 million originations in 2022 and just 4.6 million in 2023.

Despite these extreme volume swings, the origination rate (percentage of applications resulting in loans) remained surprisingly stable around 75%, suggesting lending standards stayed consistent throughout market turbulence.

Different types of lenders developed specialized niches. Non-bank mortgage companies dominate home purchases (66.1%) and cash-out refinances (67.3%). Banks maintain strength in home improvement loans (48.2%) and home equity loans (42.6%).

Recent policy changes affected investor confidence, pushing bond yields higher and increasing mortgage costs. The 30-year fixed mortgage rate peaked at nearly 8% in late 2023 before moderating to around 6.8% by early 2025.

Hispanic borrowers exceeded their population share in mortgage lending for the first time in 2024 at 17.7%. Black borrower participation remains well below population at 8.9%. Persistent gaps demand sustained action to expand access. https://ncrc.org/mortgage-market-part-1 #justeconomy #mortgagemarket #fairlending #homeownership #racialjustice
